Why I'm Building This

I'm a fan of "ship it fast." I shipped FreelancerHealth in 60 days—a tax calculator that implements the IRS Publication 974 iterative method for self-employed health insurance deductions.

The product works. Users love the logic. The math is bulletproof.

But after shipping, I discovered the go-to-market (GTM) strategy requires:

  • • 18 months of trust building (I can do this, but it drains me)
  • • CPA partnership development (I'm uncomfortable with networking)
  • • Demo calls with tax firms (I actively avoid this)

I didn't waste those 60 days—I learned a ton about tax logic, iterative calculations, and what users need.

I just wish I'd known the GTM requirements BEFORE I committed.

Not to avoid building. But to go in with realistic expectations—or to choose a different distribution strategy from day one.

"Ship fast" is great advice. This tool just asks:
"Do you know what you're signing up for AFTER you ship?"

Three Questions Before You Ship

1. What does the GTM require?

Not just "can you build it" but "how will you sell it?"

  • • Trust building timeline?
  • • Sales calls per week?
  • • Content volume needed?
  • • Partnership complexity?
  • • Social media grind?

2. What will YOU do?

Honest about your work style

  • • What energizes you?
  • • What drains you?
  • • What will you avoid?
  • • What's sustainable?

3. Where's the mismatch?

Clear picture before you commit

  • • GTM/persona gaps
  • • Realistic timelines
  • • Alternative strategies
  • • Ship knowing this

Not "Don't ship." But "Ship with clear eyes about what comes next."
