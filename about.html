About

Will I Hate This? exists for one reason: Most founders discover misalignment after they've already paid for it in time, energy, and burnout.

Startup advice usually focuses on:

  • market size
  • validation
  • execution

It rarely asks the more dangerous question:

What will this idea require from you, every week, for months?
And will you actually tolerate that?

Why This Exists

I previously built FreelancerHealth, a diagnostic tool that helps freelancers understand the real cost of health insurance before tax season.

The core insight was simple:

Systems can be technically correct and still feel awful to live inside.

Freelancers don't fail because they can't calculate. They fail because the consequences arrive late.

Founders face the same problem.

Most startup misery doesn't come from bad ideas. It comes from GTM paths that quietly conflict with how the founder works — discovered 60–90 days in, after commitment.

What This Tool Does

Will I Hate This? is a pre-mortem diagnostic.

It does not predict success. It does not give advice.

It classifies:

  • the structural go-to-market requirements of your idea
  • your actual working preferences and tolerances

And flags where those two are likely to collide.

The output is a fixed assessment:

  • GTM shape
  • misery risk level
  • estimated time-to-regret
  • specific mismatch signals

Same inputs. Same verdict.

What This Tool Is Not

  • Not coaching
  • Not validation
  • Not motivation
  • Not encouragement

It will not tell you what to do.

It only tells you what founders like you typically experience when they build ideas like this.

Philosophy

Ambiguity is expensive

Uncertainty compounds. Clarity reduces waste.

Discomfort is information

The signal matters more than the comfort.

Time is the real cost

You can recover money. You cannot recover months.

This tool exists to surface that cost early — before you pay it unknowingly.
