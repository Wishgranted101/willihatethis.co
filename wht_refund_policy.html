Refund Policy

Last Updated: January 11, 2026

No refunds for disagreement with the diagnostic verdict.

This refund policy exists to set clear expectations about when refunds are and are not issued.

The Core Principle

Will I Hate This? is a deterministic diagnostic tool, not subjective consulting. The same inputs always produce the same output.

If you disagree with your diagnostic verdict, that disagreement is not grounds for a refund. The diagnostic does not tell you what you want to hear. It classifies misalignment risk based on structural patterns.

Before purchasing, ask yourself:

If the diagnostic flags high misery risk, will I actually use that information? Or will I ignore it and want my money back?

If the answer is "ignore it," save your $37.

When Refunds ARE Issued

Refunds are issued ONLY for technical failures that prevent you from receiving or accessing your diagnostic report.

Examples of refundable technical failures:

  • You completed payment but never received your diagnostic report (on-screen or via email)
  • The assessment system failed to save your responses
  • The report generation system malfunctioned
  • You were charged but did not receive access to the assessment
  • A technical error prevented you from completing the assessment after payment

In these cases, contact support@willihatethis.com within 7 days of purchase with details of the technical failure. We will investigate and issue a full refund if the failure is confirmed.

When Refunds Are NOT Issued

Refunds are NOT issued for:

  • Disagreement with the verdict — "I don't think I'll hate this" is not grounds for a refund
  • Unexpected results — "I thought it would say something different" is not grounds for a refund
  • Unfavorable classification — "It says high misery risk but I disagree" is not grounds for a refund
  • Changed mind — "I decided not to pursue this idea" is not grounds for a refund
  • Buyer's remorse — "I regret purchasing" is not grounds for a refund
  • Inaccurate inputs — If you provided inaccurate responses, that does not entitle you to a refund
  • Misunderstood the service — The service description clearly states what the diagnostic does and does not do
  • Expected different format — The diagnostic is delivered as described (PDF + on-screen)
  • Wanted consulting instead — This is a diagnostic tool, not consulting

Why This Policy Exists

This policy exists for three reasons:

1. The diagnostic is deterministic.
Same inputs → same verdict. There is no subjective interpretation. If you don't like the verdict, that doesn't make it wrong.

2. The service is clearly described.
The landing page, about page, and FAQ explicitly state what this diagnostic does and does not do. You are purchasing a classification system, not validation or encouragement.

3. Information has value even when uncomfortable.
The most valuable diagnostic verdicts are often the ones founders don't want to hear. A refund policy based on disagreement would incentivize ignoring useful signals.

Refund Request Process

If you experienced a genuine technical failure, contact us within 7 days:

Email: support@willihatethis.com

Subject line: "Refund Request – Technical Failure"

Include:

  • Purchase date and transaction ID
  • Email address used for purchase
  • Detailed description of the technical failure
  • Screenshots if applicable

We will investigate your claim and respond within 48 hours. If the technical failure is confirmed, we will issue a full refund within 5 business days.

Chargebacks

If you initiate a chargeback without first contacting support about a technical failure, you will be permanently banned from the service.

Chargeback abuse is fraud.

If you have a legitimate technical issue, contact us. We will resolve it fairly. Filing a chargeback for disagreement with the verdict is not acceptable.

Questions About This Policy

If you have questions about this refund policy, email support@willihatethis.com before purchasing.

We are transparent about what refunds are and are not issued for. If you're uncertain about whether you'll accept the diagnostic verdict, do not purchase.
